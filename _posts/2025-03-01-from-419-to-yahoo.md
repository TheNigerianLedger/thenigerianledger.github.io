Nigeria has become a global hotspot for fraud, evolving from **419 email scams** to modern-day **crypto fraud**.

## 1️⃣ The 419 Era (1980s – Early 2000s)
419 fraud, named after **Section 419 of the Nigerian Criminal Code**, refers to **advance fee scams**—where victims are promised huge sums of money in return for an upfront "processing" or "tax" fee.

💡 **Example of a 419 scam message:**  
> "Dear Sir, I am the son of a deceased Nigerian general. I have $50M locked in a foreign account and need your help transferring it. You will receive 10%. Please send $5,000 for legal fees."

**🚨 Notable Case:**  
- One of the **largest 419 scams** involved **Emmanuel Nwude**, who **sold a fake airport to a Brazilian bank for $242 million**.  

---

## 2️⃣ The Yahoo Boys Take Over (2000s – 2015)
Fraudsters transitioned to **romance scams and identity theft**.  
*Yahoo Boys* became a **pop culture phenomenon**, even referenced in music.  

🎶 **Pop Culture Influence:**  
- Artists like **Naira Marley and Zlatan** made references to Yahoo Boys in their music.  
- Fraudsters started using **ritual sacrifices (Yahoo Plus)** to gain supernatural success.  

---

## 3️⃣ The Crypto & Tech Scam Era (2016 – Present)
By the **mid-2010s**, financial fraud evolved into **forex, crypto scams, and Ponzi schemes**.  

🔹 **Fake Crypto Investment Platforms** – Scammers create **bogus trading websites**, showing fake profits to lure victims.  
🔹 **Pump & Dump Crypto Coins** – Fraudsters hype up **scam coins**, make millions, then crash the project.  

🚨 **Case Study: MMM Ponzi Scheme**  
- The Russian Ponzi scheme **MMM** promised **30% monthly returns**.  
- Over **3 million Nigerians** lost money when it collapsed in 2017.

✔️ **AI deepfakes to impersonate real people**  
✔️ **Hackers stealing bank details through phishing sites**
